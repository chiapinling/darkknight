The Dark Knight Blueprint · Training Philosophy
Source: Logan Hood Protocol
Functional Fighter's Body
Logan Hood trained Bale using functional compound movements, gymnastic strength work, and combat conditioning. No machine isolation — every exercise had to translate to real-world athletic performance. The physique was built by moving like an athlete, not posing like one.
Source: Mark Twight / Gym Jones Methodology
Mental Hardness = Physical Results
Twight's philosophy underpins the entire block: the mind fails before the body. Density training, timed circuits, and deliberately uncomfortable rest intervals force adaptation not just physically but psychologically. You become the character by training like it.
Source: G. Joujon-Roche / Hollywood Physique
The V-Taper Cape Effect
Joujon-Roche's methodology targeted the wide-back, capped-shoulder, narrow-waist silhouette — what the bat suit amplifies. High volume weighted pull-ups, overhead pressing, and cable work build the visual architecture that makes the costume look earned, not padded.
Training Split Rationale
Push / Pull / Legs → Athletic Circuits
Phase 2A uses hypertrophy-focused PPL to build the structural mass. Phase 2B transitions to power and explosiveness — Olympic variations, plyometrics. Phase 2C introduces Batman Combat Circuits — full-body density blocks that build the athletic leanness of a real Dark Knight.
Nutrition Shift: Controlled Lean Bulk
Build Without Blurring
Phase 1 was a deficit. Phase 2 is a +200–300 kcal surplus on training days, maintenance on rest days. The goal: build muscle without allowing body fat to climb above 14%. Protein stays at 2.2g/kg. Carb timing around training becomes critical.
The Dark Knight Standard
What You're Building Toward
Bale's Dark Knight physique: ~78–80 kg, ~8–10% body fat, dominant upper back and shoulders, visible abs at rest, explosive pulling strength. Not the biggest man in the room — the most capable. At program end: that is what you will be.

12-Week Program Architecture
Weeks 1–4 · 1 Aug – 30 Aug
Phase 2A: The Foundation

Hypertrophy-focused Push/Pull/Legs split. High volume, controlled tempo, progressive overload on compounds. Build the structural muscle mass that the Batman physique requires. Slight caloric surplus. This is construction — the scaffold that everything else hangs on.

Weeks 5–8 · 31 Aug – 27 Sep
Phase 2B: The Power Block

Explosive power development. Olympic lift variations (hang cleans, power snatches), plyometrics, and combat conditioning introduced. Strength work transitions to heavier, lower-rep clusters. This is where the physique stops looking built and starts looking dangerous.

Weeks 9–12 · 28 Sep – 25 Oct
Phase 2C: The Batman Circuit

Logan Hood-style full-body density circuits. Timed blocks, minimal rest, maximum output. Strength maintained. Conditioning peaks. The final 4 weeks reveal everything built in Phases 2A and 2B — a lean, powerful, athletic physique that earns the Dark Knight standard.

⚡ Kickoff Day — Saturday 1 Aug 2026

The program officially launches Saturday 1 August. This is not a training day — it is your assessment and orientation session. Complete the following before Week 1 begins on Monday 3 August.

  • Morning weigh-in (fasted). Record weight, take progress photos (front/side/back). Re-scan BIA if possible.
  • Test 1RM or estimated 1RM for Squat, Bench, Deadlift, and max bodyweight pull-ups. Log all numbers.
  • Time yourself on 10 pull-ups + 20 push-ups + 20 air squats + 400m run. This is your baseline conditioning score.
  • Set up nutrition tracking. Calculate TDEE at new bodyweight (~72 kg). Training day surplus target: +250 kcal.
  • Read the entire 12-week program. Know Phase 2A before Monday. Prepare your mind. The Dark Knight does not improvise.
