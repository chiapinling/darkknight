Coach's Notes — The Standard

On the philosophy: Logan Hood, the former Navy SEAL who trained Christian Bale for The Dark Knight, built that physique on three principles — functional strength that translates to movement, explosive power that makes a body look athletic rather than static, and conditioning that keeps fat at bay without sacrificing muscle. Every phase of this protocol is built on those three pillars, scaled precisely to your numbers.

On the weighted pull-up: It is the single most visible indicator of the lat and bicep development that defines this silhouette. Protect those sets. Never trade form for load.

On the signature circuit: The AMRAP format is deliberate. It removes the option of pacing — you either work or you don't. The round count is the proof. By Week 12 you should complete 2–3 more rounds than Week 9 at heavier loads. That is adaptation made quantifiable.

On identity: The mask doesn't make you. The work does. Twelve weeks of consistent, relentless action will change more than your body — it changes what you believe you're capable of.
