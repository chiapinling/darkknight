The Dark Knight Blueprint · Training Philosophy
Source: Logan Hood Protocol
Functional Fighter's Body
Logan Hood trained Bale using functional compound movements, gymnastic strength work, and combat conditioning. No machine isolation — every exercise had to translate to real-world athletic performance. The physique was built by moving like an athlete, not posing like one.
Source: Mark Twight / Gym Jones Methodology
Mental Hardness = Physical Results
Twight's philosophy underpins the entire block: the mind fails before the body. Density training, timed circuits, and deliberately uncomfortable rest intervals force adaptation not just physically but psychologically. You become the character by training like it.
Source: G. Joujon-Roche / Hollywood Physique
The V-Taper Cape Effect
Joujon-Roche's methodology targeted the wide-back, capped-shoulder, narrow-waist silhouette — what the bat suit amplifies. High volume weighted pull-ups, overhead pressing, and cable work build the visual architecture that makes the costume look earned, not padded.
Training Split Rationale
3 Lifts + 1 Hill Run + 1 Optional
Phase 1 verdict accepted: four lifting days in a deficit was too much fatigue. The fix is three harder sessions with real recovery — Tue Lower · Thu Upper · Sun Full Body — plus the Saturday 5K hill run (Z4–5) and an optional Friday (pump / Z2 / golf) earned with two nights of 7+ hour sleep. Post-lift fat-burn finishers: 15-min Zone 2 incline walk, never HIIT.
Nutrition Shift: The Cut
A Deficit That Actually Exists
Phase 1's deficit never existed — ~300 phantom kcal/day of unlogged oil and sauce. Phase 2 fixes the system: fat capped at 60 g, everything weighed, ±100 kcal band. Lift days 2,150 · run day 2,000 · rest days 1,850 → a true ~400 kcal/day deficit. Protein holds at 180 g — more was never the lever.

12-Week Program Architecture
Weeks 1–4 · 3 Aug – 30 Aug
Phase 2A: Hypertrophy Foundation

Moderate loads, honest volume, full range. Re-establish the rhythm and validate the nutrition numbers before anything fancy. Progression: +2.5 kg or +1 rep per week only if last week's top set was ≤ RPE 8. Week 4 drops the last set of everything — a half-deload before the power block. CP1 (W2) is the checkpoint that matters: if the scale hasn't moved, audit the log — never cut calories.

Weeks 5–8 · 31 Aug – 27 Sep · Bali W6
Phase 2B: Power & Olympic

Heavier, faster, lower reps — bar speed is the metric. Week 6 is Bali (6–11 Sep): a scheduled deload, not a lost week. One hotel-gym session, 10k+ steps daily, protein anchored 160–180 g, no scale until Wed 17 Sep. W7 restarts at W5 loads; W8 pushes past them into the strength audit.

Weeks 9–12 · 28 Sep – 25 Oct
Phase 2C: Batman Density

More work per minute — EMOMs, supersets, the Sunday Combat Circuit against the clock. Loads drop slightly; the stopwatch becomes the opponent, exactly when the deficit is deepest and the body lightest. Week 12 tapers into the final DEXA + photos (24–25 Oct), then a 2-week maintenance diet break.

⚡ Ramp Weekend — Sat 1 & Sun 2 Aug 2026

The phase launches with a setup weekend, not a training day. Week 1 begins Monday 3 August with the baseline scan.

  • Grocery run + meal prep for the three day-types: lift 2,150 · run 2,000 · rest 1,850 kcal. Buy the food scale batteries. Oil goes on a spoon, sauces on the side, weighed.
  • Set up the log with the ±100 kcal band. Phase 1 swung 1,559–2,897 kcal — a 2,400 day is not "fixed" by a 1,600 day; both are misses.
  • Mon 3 Aug baseline BIA under standardized conditions: same weekday, fasted, pre-training, post-bathroom, no alcohol or high sodium in the prior 24h. Photos front/side/back.
  • Walk or drive the Saturday hill-run route once. Know where the climb bites.
  • Read Weeks 1–4 in full. RPE cap 8.5 on all barbell work — grinding reps in a deficit buys fatigue, not muscle.

On why this is a cut: The original Phase 2 was a lean bulk written for a man arriving at 12%. The data said you arrived at 22.6% with the fat mass untouched — a surplus from here would have ended near 25–27% by October. The plan changed because the facts did. On three sessions instead of four: Your fatigue feedback was data, not weakness. Four lifting days on 2,000 kcal grinds a man down and calls it discipline. Three sessions trained fully present, with real recovery between them, beat four trained at half voltage — every time.

On the post-lift finishers: Fifteen minutes of Zone 2 incline walking after lifting adds ~120–150 kcal of output at zero recovery cost. That is the entire pitch. The deficit does the fat burning — the finisher just widens it. Anyone selling you post-lift HIIT in a deficit is selling you a strength audit failure at Week 8.

On the hill run: One 5K at +168 m in Zone 4–5 per week is the correct dose — the engine stays sharp without eating the recovery budget.
